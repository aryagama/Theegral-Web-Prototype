Mulakma
Tondi

Debora Violin

A story of a Batak child, far from the land of his ancestral tribe, North Sumatra. Was born, living amidst the hustle and bustle of Bandung, the capital city of West Java. She accidentally hears "gondang", which propels her into a whirlwind of suppressed emotions and revives her memories of the Batak Toba philosophy of life.
The disconnection from her roots in the present raises many questions about the worthiness. Has she done enough? Is there a scripture that defines the essence of a Batak? The geographical, sociocultural, and behavioral differences between her cultural identity and her current environment are indeed stark. A sense of floating, alienation, strangeness, conflict, disorder, and a lack of grounding emerge. This is a portrayal of her frozen shape and moment in time, leading her back to the family wisdom and oral traditions passed down through generations.

"Gir-gir manginang-inangi bakol ma hatahon" Shall every mouth swallow its saliva and widen their ears to hear.
